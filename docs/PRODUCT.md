# Product Concept

## Purpose

This document explains the product concept.

It should help any developer or AI assistant quickly understand what the platform is, why it exists, and what problem it solves.

This document focuses on product vision rather than implementation.

---

# Product Summary

The platform is a SaaS workspace for professionals who provide services to clients.

Examples include:

- psychologists
- tutors
- consultants
- lawyers
- accountants
- coaches
- dietitians
- trainers
- therapists
- mentors

The platform is designed for professionals who sell their time, knowledge, or expertise.

---

# The Problem

Most professionals manage their work using many disconnected tools.

Typical workflow today:

- Instagram or Telegram for communication.
- Google Calendar for scheduling.
- Google Meet or Zoom for calls.
- Google Drive for files.
- Notes in Notion or paper.
- Payments through external services.

As a result:

- information is scattered;
- booking is manual;
- files get lost;
- important notes disappear in chats;
- client history is difficult to follow.

---

# The Solution

The platform combines the most important parts of this workflow into one place.

Instead of switching between multiple tools, specialists manage client work through one structured workspace.

The platform does not try to replace every existing service.

Instead, it becomes the central place where client work is organized.

---

# Core Concept

The most important concept of the platform is the **Session Workspace**.

Every booking creates its own dedicated workspace.

That workspace contains everything related to one session:

- booking information;
- client information;
- meeting link;
- messages;
- materials;
- files;
- notes (future);
- archive.

The session is the core object of the entire platform.

Everything else exists to support the session.

---

# What Makes the Platform Different

Most booking systems stop after the appointment is created.

Our platform continues supporting the specialist before, during, and after the session.

Instead of simply creating calendar events, it creates an organized workspace for collaboration.

This makes the platform useful throughout the entire client relationship rather than only during scheduling.

---

# Product Philosophy

The platform follows several principles.

## Simplicity

The product should remain simple.

Avoid unnecessary complexity.

Avoid enterprise-style interfaces.

---

## Session First

Every important feature should improve the session workflow.

If a feature does not help professionals manage client sessions better, it probably does not belong in the MVP.

---

## Not a Marketplace

The platform is NOT a marketplace.

Professionals already find clients elsewhere.

The platform helps them work with those clients.

---

## Structured Information

Important information should not disappear inside chat history.

Materials should be stored separately from messages whenever possible.

---

## Professional Experience

The interface should feel:

- clean;
- modern;
- calm;
- trustworthy;
- premium.

It should never feel like a social network.

---

# Main Modules

The MVP includes:

- Public Profile
- Services
- Availability
- Booking
- Dashboard
- Session Workspace
- Chat
- Materials
- Archive
- Settings

---

# Future Direction

Possible future additions include:

- payments;
- notifications;
- analytics;
- mobile application;
- integrations;
- AI assistant.

These features should only be added if they strengthen the main workflow.

---

# MVP Goal

The goal of the MVP is to validate that professionals want one place to manage the complete lifecycle of client sessions.

The MVP is successful if a specialist can:

- create a profile;
- publish services;
- receive bookings;
- open a session workspace;
- communicate with the client;
- share materials;
- archive completed sessions.

---

# Out of Scope

The MVP will not include:

- marketplace discovery;
- built-in video calls;
- enterprise CRM features;
- team management;
- advanced analytics;
- AI automation;
- complex workflow automation.

---

# Final Principle

Every future feature should answer one question:

"Does this improve the way specialists work with clients?"

If the answer is no, the feature should probably not be part of the product.
